<h2 style="margin-bottom:20px;">Why Marker?</h2>
<div style="border-left:3px solid var(--color-brand, #1a7a4a);padding:14px 20px;margin-bottom:28px;background:rgba(0,0,0,0.03);border-radius:0 4px 4px 0;">
<p style="font-size:0.7rem;font-weight:700;letter-spacing:0.12em;text-transform:uppercase;margin:0 0 6px;opacity:0.55;">MARKER &mdash; noun</p>
<p style="margin:0;font-style:italic;font-size:0.95rem;">In stroke play, the person responsible for recording a player's scores and certifying the scorecard.</p>
</div>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">In competitive golf, a marker is more than a scorekeeper. The marker records another player's scores and, at the end of the round, helps certify the accuracy of the scorecard.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">It's a role built into the traditions and rules of competitive golf: one player keeping the record for another, creating a shared and trusted account of what happened on the course.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">That idea inspired our name.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">Marker brings that tradition to the modern game. It records the scores, tracks the games within the game, applies handicaps, updates leaderboards, and keeps the group working from the same result.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:0;">The tools have changed. The idea hasn't: play the game, keep the record, and know where everyone stands.</p>

<h2 style="margin-bottom:20px;">The story</h2>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">Every golf group has the same post-round ritual: huddle around someone's phone, squint at the scorecard, argue about whether the press was two holes or three, and hope the guy who carries a calculator in his bag is still around.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">We made Marker so that moment could be instant instead of painful. Set up your games on the first tee, keep score on any device, and walk off 18 knowing exactly who owes who — without the mental math.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:18px;">Marker is designed for the way real groups actually play: mixed handicaps, multiple side games running simultaneously, commissioners managing leagues, and the one guy who always forgets to bring cash.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom:48px;">We're a small team and we play every week. Marker is the app we always wanted.</p>
