<p>Marker gives a group one shared scorecard. Each player enters scores on their own phone and everyone sees the same grid in real time. Add side games — Skins, Nassau, Stroke Play, and more — and Marker calculates standings and accounting from the same entered scores, updating as the round progresses.</p>

<p>For larger outings with multiple groups, a Marker Event organizes everything under a shared leaderboard.</p>

<h3>Sign in</h3>

<p>Marker uses your US phone number as your account identity — no password required.</p>

<ol>
<li>Open Marker and enter your mobile number.</li>
<li>Tap <strong>Submit</strong>. A six-digit verification code arrives by text message.</li>
<li>Enter the code and tap <strong>Login</strong>. You're signed in.</li>
</ol>

<p>Your phone number is how other players find and add you in Marker. If someone else is setting up your round, they may text you a link to the shared scorecard — open the link and sign in the same way.</p>

<p>Marker is available as a native app for iPhone and Android, and as a web app at <strong>app.marker.golf</strong> — no download required.</p>

<h3>The Home screen</h3>

<p>After signing in, you land on the Home screen. The green area at the top shows your name. If you've connected your USGA Handicap ID, it also displays your current Handicap Index, Low H.I., club name, and GHIN number. If you haven't connected a USGA ID yet, a <strong>Connect USGA ID</strong> button appears in its place — that setup is covered in Set Up Your Profile, the next tutorial in this path.</p>

<p>Below the green hero, the Home screen has two action tiles and two recent-history lists.</p>

<h3>New Game or Host Event?</h3>

<p>The two action tiles represent Marker's two main modes.</p>

<p><strong>New Game · 1–5 players</strong><br>
Creates a standalone scorecard for one group. Up to five players share a single scoring grid, and any player can enter or correct scores from their own phone. This is the right choice for a regular foursome, a casual round, or any time one group is playing together.</p>

<p>The <a href="/tutorials/create-a-scorecard/">Playing with Your Group</a> tutorial path walks through every step — create a scorecard, add players, configure games, score the round, and settle up.</p>

<p><strong>Host Event · 6+ players</strong><br>
Organizes a larger outing under a single event. An event supports multiple groups, each with its own scorecard, and brings results together in a shared leaderboard. This is the right choice when you have more players than fit on one scorecard, or when you're running a structured outing with tee times or multiple flights.</p>

<p>The <a href="/tutorials/create-an-event/">Running an Event</a> tutorial path covers the full event workflow.</p>

<p>Not sure which to use: if everyone playing fits on one scorecard (up to five players), use <strong>New Game</strong>. If you have multiple groups or need a combined leaderboard, use <strong>Host Event</strong>.</p>

<h3>Recent Scorecards and Events</h3>

<p>Below the action tiles, the Home screen lists your three most recent scorecards and three most recent events. Tap any item to reopen it. Use <strong>See all</strong> next to either section to view your full history. There is no separate statistics or history dashboard — scorecards and events are the record of your play in Marker.</p>
