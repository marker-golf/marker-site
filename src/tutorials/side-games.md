<div class="tutorial-step">
<div class="tutorial-step__marker" aria-hidden="true">1</div>
<div class="tutorial-step__content">
<h3>What is a side game?</h3>
<p>A side game is a smaller game created from an individual event scorecard. It is separate from the event-wide games that the organizer configures — players can add their own side bets independently, without involving the whole field.</p>
<p>A side game can include up to five players, and those players can be on different scorecards. For example: two players in one group might run a Nassau against two players in a different group. Each player enters scores on their own event scorecard, and Marker uses those entered scores to track the side game result.</p>
<p>Multiple side games can run simultaneously on the same scorecard. Each tracks and settles independently.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="tutorial-step">
<div class="tutorial-step__marker" aria-hidden="true">2</div>
<div class="tutorial-step__content">
<h3>Open the scorecard info screen</h3>
<p>From your event scorecard, tap the <strong>ⓘ icon</strong> in the top-right corner. This opens the scorecard info screen, which shows the players on the scorecard, the active games, and scorecard settings. Games are added and managed from here.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="tutorial-step">
<div class="tutorial-step__marker" aria-hidden="true">3</div>
<div class="tutorial-step__content">
<h3>Add a game</h3>
<p>Scroll to the <strong>Games</strong> section on the info screen and tap the <strong>+</strong> button. The game picker opens — the same picker used for event-wide games, listing all 11 available formats. Tap any format to open its configuration form.</p>
<p>The <strong>+</strong> button is only visible while the scorecard is unlocked. If the round has already been ended, tap <strong>Unlock</strong> on the info screen to make changes.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="tutorial-step">
<div class="tutorial-step__marker" aria-hidden="true">4</div>
<div class="tutorial-step__content">
<h3>Choose players</h3>
<p>The <strong>Players</strong> section of the configuration form lists the full event roster, not just your local group. Players on your own scorecard are selected by default. From there:</p>
<ul>
<li>Deselect any local players who are not part of this bet.</li>
<li>Select players from other event scorecards to include them as participants.</li>
<li>Side games support a maximum of five participants total.</li>
</ul>
<p>This is what makes side games different from event-wide games: you choose a specific subset of players across the field, not the full roster.</p>
<p>After selecting players, configure handicap settings and any format-specific options — stakes, carry rules, team setup — below the player list. Tap <strong>Save game</strong> to finish.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="tutorial-step">
<div class="tutorial-step__marker" aria-hidden="true">5</div>
<div class="tutorial-step__content">
<h3>Track results and settle</h3>
<p>Side game results appear on the scorecard leaderboard. Tap the leaderboard icon from your scorecard to open it. The game section is labeled <strong>Games</strong> for standalone rounds, or <strong>Side Games</strong> when event-wide games are also active on the same scorecard.</p>
<p>The settlement panel below the standings shows <strong>Day total</strong> for standalone rounds, or <strong>Side games total</strong> when event games are also present. Results are based on the scores each participant has entered on their own scorecard and become final when the scorecard is locked.</p>
<p>Side game settlement is entirely separate from the event leaderboard. Event Games settle under <strong>Event total</strong> on the event leaderboard — side game winnings and losses are kept on the scorecard leaderboard and are not included in that total.</p>
</div>
</div>

</div>

<div class="tutorial-note">
<p><strong>Side games vs. Event Games:</strong> Event Games are added by the organizer from the event dashboard and appear on every participant's leaderboard across the whole field. Side games are added by players from their own scorecard and appear only on that scorecard's leaderboard — they are the right tool for group-specific bets that the organizer doesn't need to manage. See <a href="{{ '/tutorials/add-and-configure-games/' | url }}">Add Event Games</a> for a walkthrough of the organizer-managed path.</p>
</div>
